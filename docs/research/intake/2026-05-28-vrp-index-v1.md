# Strategy Intake — VRP Index v1

## 1. Strategy name

`vrp_index_v1`

One-line: Index-level variance-risk-premium timing on SPY and QQQ using
free CBOE-derived volatility indices.

## 2. Hypothesis statement

Index option markets price implied variance higher than realized
variance on average because long-vol hedgers (insurance buyers,
asymmetric-return funds, vol-targeting strategies that need to reduce
risk during drawdowns) pay a structural premium to vol-sellers. The
short-vol seller collects this variance risk premium (VRP) in normal
regimes but bears the crash risk during volatility spikes (Bondarenko
2014, Carr & Wu 2009).

This is a fundamentally different information channel from price/volume:
the VRP is the option market's forward-looking view of variance
*relative to* what subsequently realizes — it cannot be reconstructed
from spot OHLCV alone.

Operational restatement: when implied variance (VIX² in annualized
form) materially exceeds trailing realized variance, the option market
is paying for crash insurance the seller does not yet have to deliver;
equity exposure should be elevated. When implied collapses to trailing
realized (or below it), the premium has been compressed and equity
exposure should be reduced. Conditioning features (VIX term structure,
VVIX, SKEW, VXN) refine the rule by capturing tail-risk pricing and
volatility-of-volatility regimes.

## 3. Information sources

- `ohlcv` — SPY, QQQ underlying bars
- `options_implied_vol` — `^VIX`, `^VIX9D`, `^VIX3M`, `^VVIX`, `^SKEW`,
  `^VXN` (CBOE indices via yfinance, daily, free)

**Non-OHLCV source declared: YES.** The strategy is therefore eligible
for `promotion_eligible` status if it survives the 8-criteria gate.

## 4. Why this is not an OHLCV restatement

VIX, VIX9D, VIX3M, VVIX, SKEW, and VXN encode the **option market's**
forward 9d-to-3m variance / skew expectations. These are quoted prices
of variance contracts (or in SKEW's case, the implied tail-risk
premium derived from out-of-the-money put prices). They are not
derivable from SPX or NDX spot prices alone — they consume bids and
offers in the option market.

Specifically, VIX² approximates a risk-neutral expectation
`E^Q[realized_variance_30d]` under the variance-swap formula
(Whaley 1993, Demeterfi-Derman-Kamal-Zou 1999). The risk-neutral
expectation differs from the physical expectation
`E^P[realized_variance_30d]` by exactly the variance risk premium.
This is the strategy's edge — if the edge exists, it lives in the gap
between Q and P measures, which is unobservable from spot price alone.

## 5. Expected gross Sharpe and capacity (pre-registered)

- **Gross Sharpe (dev):** 0.7-1.2 (paper-documented VRP magnitude on SPX
  is ~0.5 net of vol-of-vol drag and crash episodes; SPY proxy adds
  no material slippage).
- **Net Sharpe (full hedge-fund costs):** 0.5-0.9 (commissions are
  negligible on SPY/QQQ; spread cost dominates and is ~0.5 bps for
  these instruments).
- **Capacity:** indicative $100M-$1B on SPY/QQQ at < 1% ADV
  participation. Not a binding constraint at our test scale.

**Decision rule:** if dev net Sharpe lands below 0.5, classify as
"VRP exists but cannot survive even index-level execution costs". If
dev Sharpe is in [0.5, 1.5] and holdout agrees within ±0.3, candidate
remains alive. If dev Sharpe > 1.5, suspect leakage — look for VIX
timestamp issues or look-ahead in the realized-variance window.

## 6. Cost assumptions

- Commission: 0.5 bps one-way (large-cap ETF, prime broker rate)
- Spread: 0.5 bps one-way for SPY/QQQ at top of book
- Borrow: 0 (long SPY/QQQ only — no shorting in v1)
- 2× and 3× cost stress
- 1-bar delay stress

Rationale: SPY/QQQ are the two most liquid US ETFs; their spreads are
materially tighter than the 1 bps default we use for SP500
single-names. Commission is unchanged.

## 7. Universe and history

- Universe: SPY, QQQ (initially SPY only; QQQ as secondary if VXN
  available)
- History: 2010-01-01 → 2026-05-26 (~16 years; VVIX starts 2007,
  ^VXN starts 2001, ^VIX9D starts 2011, ^VIX3M starts 2007)
- Dev: 2010-01-01 → 2022-12-31 (~13 years; spans 2011 EU crisis,
  2013 taper tantrum, 2014 oil collapse, 2015-16 commodity bust,
  2018 vol shock, 2020 COVID, 2022 bear)
- Holdout: 2023-01-01 → 2026-05-26 (~3.4 years; 2023-25 AI rally +
  2024 vol uptick + 2025 trade-policy episodes)

The dev window deliberately covers multiple regimes so concentration-
by-month diagnostic catches "edge dominated by 2020 March" or similar.

## 8. Pre-registered failure modes

Three specific failures the proposer expects:

1. **"VRP works but is killed by single-event concentration."**
   Short-vol strategies notoriously make slow money for years and lose
   it all in March 2020 / Feb 2018 / Dec 2018. The concentration
   diagnostic should fire if any single month carries > 50% of |PnL|.
   Pre-registered: max_month_share > 0.5 → `single_period_dominance`
   failure class.

2. **"Term-structure conditioning is just risk-on/risk-off and is
   already priced."** If `vrp_with_term_structure` performs no better
   than `vrp_long_only`, the term-structure feature adds nothing.
   Pre-registered: variant-grid PBO should be > 0.20 if all variants
   are duplicates of each other.

3. **"SKEW/VVIX features are noisy and inflate the variant grid."**
   If the combined-VRP variant fails to beat the simplest VRP-only
   variant, the additional features are degrees of freedom that DSR
   should penalize. Pre-registered: DSR for best variant should
   drop sharply if the best is `vrp_combined`.

## 9. Promotion intent

`research_only` for v1. The strategy is intended to test whether a
genuinely new information channel produces alpha after the six-
iteration OHLCV failure. If it survives, future iterations may propose
`paper_trade_after_pass`.

## Variant grid (predeclared)

Six variants in the PBO/DSR pool, plus three baselines:

**VRP variants:**
1. `vrp_long_only` — long SPY when `VRP > 0` (i.e. IV² > RV²); flat otherwise
2. `vrp_long_short` — long SPY when `VRP > 0`; short SPY when `VRP < 0`
3. `vrp_with_term_structure` — variant 1 AND `VIX9D < VIX` (contango = risk-on)
4. `vrp_with_vvix` — variant 1 AND `VVIX/VIX` below trailing-60d median
5. `vrp_with_skew` — variant 1 AND `SKEW` below trailing-60d median
6. `vrp_combined` — variant 1 with a weighted z-score of all conditioning features

**Baselines:**
- `spy_buy_and_hold` — always long SPY
- `hmm_only_gate` — HMM regime on SPY returns, long SPY only in risk-on state
- `mom_12_1_spy` — 12-1 momentum on SPY-as-single-asset (timing version)

9 strategies in PBO/DSR pool.

## Cost-decomposition expectation

VRP is a relatively low-turnover strategy (positions change on vol-regime
shifts, not daily). Expect:
- `no_cost` Sharpe close to `full_cost` Sharpe (small spread between them)
- `stress_2×` Sharpe close to `full_cost` (since costs are already small)
- `1-bar delay` Sharpe close to `full_cost` (regime signal doesn't decay in one day)

If turnover-driven costs dominate, the strategy was an OHLCV strategy in
disguise.
